The importance of finding a balanced study load

The study load's effects on a student's well being and success

A student's well being is often overlooked and never analyzed. Study load plays an important factor in the well being of students and we should determine the extents of how beneficial a high study load is compared to a lower study load.

Ian is a hard working student in high school who would rate his study load a 5 out of 5.

However, Ian would disagree that, with this large amount of study load, he excels in his classes. He would rate his academic performance a 2 out of 5.

Ian also rates his depression level a 20 out of 30, his self esteem a 10 out of 30, and his anxiety level a 16 out of 30.

Now, these numbers aren't actually random, and are actually the averages of the students who rate their study load a 5 out of 5.

These aren't great numbers for any student, and it tells a story how a high study load can hurt the studen't not only academically, but also hurts their emotions and phsycological personality.

That begs the question: How does the experience of a student's study load contribute to shaping a comprehensive profile of a student's overall success and well-being?
What is the right balance?

Let's take a look at how the academic performance is affected by the study load.

Student's who rate their study load a 1 out of 5 have a higher rated average academic performance.

Compared to the 4-5 range, this is a drastic difference!

The negative impact that a high study load also carries into the many other variables of physcological factors of the student aswell!

On the other hand, other study load averages, particularly the 3-5 range, report numbers on the opposite side of the overall average.

How can we use this information to improve students' well-being?

Professors need to be aware of the amount of work that they give their students. If you are an educator, consider getting your students' opinions on how much study load you give out. If they report on the higher ends, it is best to adjust lower work loads and remember to teach your student's efficiently and effectively. Aim for an average study load rating of 1 and possibly 2.

No matter what role you play in the educational system, as a student, you can advocate to an efficient and non-harmful study load.
